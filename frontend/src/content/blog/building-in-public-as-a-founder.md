When I started **2 AM Study**, I had no playbook. I had handwritten notes, a Telegram group, and a genuine desire to help students who felt as lost as I once did.

Instead of waiting until everything was perfect, I shared the journey — the wins, the failures, the late-night debugging sessions, the moments when growth stalled.

Students didn't just buy products. They bought into the story. They became advocates because they watched it happen in real time.

Building in public isn't about oversharing. It's about being honest enough that people trust you when you say something will help them learn.
